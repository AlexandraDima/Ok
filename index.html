Living&working in Denmark

Quick steps

FIND ACCOMODATION

Denmark doesn't have a tradition for on-campus hosting. Most students live together in residences situated some distance from the campus.

OFFICIAL RESIDENCE

To be easier for you to work and live in Denmark, you will need to first fill some official forms. You need to get a CPR number from the Communes.

HEALTH INSURANCE

Denmark offers free healthcare for the international students. Here you can find more information about how you are covered during your studies.

Find a job

If you are a student and if you work the required hours to pay enough taxes as a foreigner in Denmark, you are able to apply and receive SU.

Tax system

The Danish welfare state is, among other things, based on the concept of citizens having equal access to the different services paid for by taxes.

Learn danish

If you have a Danish CPR number, free Danish courses are often available as an online activity at language school.

Guide to citizens from EU and EEA countries, who wish to apply for admission to English based undergraduate programmes in Denmark. For applicants from outside EU/EEA special rules apply.

Tuition fees

Higher education in Denmark is free for students from the EU/EEA and Switzerland. Similarly, if you are participating in an exchange programme your studies in Denmark are free.

View more

Study options

Danish higher education institutions offer a range of opportunities for international students. More than 600 programmes are taught in English - all internationally recognised and of high quality

View more

Teaching style

Danish higher education features innovative teaching methods and an informal learning environment designed to promote creativity, self-expression, analytical and critical thinking

Why studying in Denmark
